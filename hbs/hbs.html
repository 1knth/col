Cottagers & Indians

BIG IDEAS

  • Reconciliaton
  • Intergenerational trauma
  • Indigenous land rights

QUOTES

“My play is called Cottagers and Indians, not Cottagers versus Indians”

‎

“Cottagers & Indians” by Drew Hayden Taylor explores the complexities surrounding land rights, cultural preservation, and reconciliation between the non-Indigenous cottagers and Indigenous communities. Throughout the start of the documentary, Taylor shows the viewer a deep-rooted and intergenerational conflict between the cottagers and Indians over land boundaries. Despite this, Taylor emphasized later the importance of the documentary’s title, “Cottagers & Indians,” to convey a message of connection and shared responsibility (Taylor 12:16). He challenges the notion of a hostile relationship between the two communities by using the word “and” instead of “versus” which implies that there is an ongoing conflict, which spreads the idea of an “us versus them” mentality (Taylor 12:16). The brilliance of this wording recognizes the idea of coexisting with one another, mutual engagement, and potential for collaboration. This inclusive approach acknowledges the need for shared responsibility in order to find common ground to work toward reconciliation. Taylor aims to tear down the barrier between the two communities and has taught me to apply the concepts of unity, shared understanding, and collaborative coexistence.
